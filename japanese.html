The Essential Guide to Japanese Phonetic Script

What is Katakana

Hiragana is one of the three primary scripts in Japanese. It's a phonetic alphabet used for native Japanese words, grammatical functions, and pronunciation. Learning Hiragana is your first step in mastering the Japanese language!

Why Learn Katakana?

  • Learning katakana is essential for reading foreign words, names, and technical terms in Japanese.
  • It’s used for loanwords, onomatopoeia, and scientific terms, as well as for emphasis and stylistic purposes.
  • Understanding katakana is key to navigating modern Japanese texts, advertisements, and everyday language.
  • Improves pronunciation and overall fluency.
